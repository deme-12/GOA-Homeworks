APOLO 11

Apollo 11 was the first mission to land humans on the Moon. Launched by NASA on July 16, 1969, it landed on the Moon on July 20, 1969, and returned to Earth on July 24, 1969.
Crew: Neil Armstrong: First person to walk on the Moon. Buzz Aldrin: Second person on the Moon. Michael Collins: Orbited the Moon in the Command Module. Highlights: Landed in the Sea of Tranquility. Armstrong's famous words: "That's one small step for [a] man, one giant leap for mankind." Collected 21.5 kg of lunar samples and deployed scientific instruments. Legacy: A historic achievement in space exploration, fulfilling President Kennedy's goal and marking a major milestone for humanity.
