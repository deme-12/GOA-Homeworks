SpaceX Falcon 9

Falcon 9 is SpaceX's workhorse rocket, designed for reliable and cost-effective missions to space. It's a two-stage rocket capable of delivering payloads to a variety of orbits, including the International Space Station (ISS). Its reusability revolutionized the industry, with the first stage capable of landing vertically for refurbishment and reuse. Notable achievements include over 250 successful launches and landings, making it one of the most reliable rockets in history. Falcon 9 has also supported high-profile missions like Crew Dragon's human flights to the ISS and numerous Starlink satellite deployments. Its innovations significantly lowered launch costs, democratizing access to space.

SpaceX Falcon Heavy

Falcon Heavy is the world's most powerful operational rocket, capable of lifting 64 metric tons to low Earth orbit (LEO). Comprising three Falcon 9 cores, it delivers exceptional payload capacity for interplanetary missions and heavy cargo. It debuted in 2018 with the launch of Elon Musk's Tesla Roadster, a milestone in aerospace history. Falcon Heavy has supported missions like the U.S. Space Force's STP-2 and classified military payloads. With partial reusability, the rocket balances power and cost-effectiveness, setting benchmarks for future heavy-lift vehicles. It's a key player for Moon and Mars exploration efforts.
