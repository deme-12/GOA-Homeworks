Leonardo da Vinci

Leonardo da Vinci (1452–1519) was an Italian polymath, renowned for his contributions to art, science, engineering, anatomy, and many other fields. He is
widely regarded as one of the greatest minds in history, embodying the ideal of the Renaissance Man—a person skilled in multiple disciplines.

Early Life Born: April 15, 1452, in Vinci, Italy. Family: Leonardo was the illegitimate son of Ser Piero da Vinci, a notary, and a peasant woman named Caterina. Education: He received informal education in Latin, geometry, and mathematics but demonstrated exceptional talent in drawing and observation from an early age.
