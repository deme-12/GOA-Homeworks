Learn More about Japan

Japan Location: East Asia, an island nation in the Pacific Ocean. Capital: Tokyo. Population: Approximately 125 million (2023). Official Language: Japanese. Currency: Japanese Yen (JPY). Government: Parliamentary constitutional monarchy. Key Features:
Known for its blend of traditional and modern culture, including ancient temples and cutting-edge technology. Famous landmarks: Mount Fuji, Kyoto temples, and Tokyo’s skyscrapers. Major industries: Electronics, automobiles (e.g., Toyota, Honda), robotics, and anime. Cuisine: Sushi, ramen, tempura, and matcha tea. Challenges: Aging population and frequent natural disasters (earthquakes, tsunamis).



Learn More about Nepal

Nepal Location: South Asia, landlocked between India and China. Capital: Kathmandu. Population: Approximately 30 million (2023). Official Language: Nepali. Currency: Nepalese Rupee (NPR). Government: Federal democratic republic. Key Features: Home to the Himalayas and Mount Everest, the world’s highest peak.
Rich cultural heritage with influences from Hinduism and Buddhism. Famous landmarks: Lumbini (Buddha’s birthplace), Pashupatinath Temple, and Annapurna Circuit. Economy: Agriculture, tourism, and remittances from workers abroad. Challenges: Poverty, infrastructure development, and vulnerability to earthquakes.



Lear More About Peru

Peru Location: Western South America, bordered by the Pacific Ocean. Capital: Lima. Population: Approximately 34 million (2023). Official Languages: Spanish (primary), Quechua, and Aymara. Currency: Peruvian Sol (PEN). Government: Unitary presidential republic. Key Features: Famous for its ancient civilizations, including the Inca Empire.
Iconic landmarks: Machu Picchu, the Sacred Valley, and the Amazon Rainforest. Diverse geography: Coastal deserts, Andes mountains, and tropical rainforests. Economy: Mining (gold, silver, copper), agriculture (coffee, quinoa), and tourism. Cuisine: Renowned for ceviche, lomo saltado, and Peruvian superfoods like quinoa and maca.
