Father of a Soldier (original title: Jariskatsis mama) is a 1964 Georgian-Russian drama film directed by Revaz Chkheidze.The film is set during World War II and tells the poignant story of a father's desperate journey to find his son, who has been wounded in battle. It explores themes of love, sacrifice, and the harsh realities of war. The story takes place in the summer of 1942, focusing on Giorgi Makharashvili, an elderly Georgian farmer portrayed by Sergo Zakariadze. After receiving news that his son Goderdzi has been wounded and is recovering in a hospital, Giorgi embarks on a journey to visit him. However, upon arrival, he learns that Goderdzi has already been discharged and sent back to the front lines.
Reluctant to return home without seeing his son, Giorgi decides to enlist in the army himself to join the fight against the Germans in hopes of finding Goderdzi. The film culminates in a tragic yet heroic moment as Giorgi attempts to rescue his son during a fierce battle, only to discover that Goderdzi has been fatally wounded.





Conclusion Father of a Soldier remains an important piece of cinematic history that reflects on the human experience during wartime. Its blend of drama and anti-war sentiment resonates with audiences, making it a memorable exploration of love and sacrifice amidst the chaos of conflict.
