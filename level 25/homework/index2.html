Titanic is a 1997 American epic romance and disaster film directed by James Cameron. The film is centered around the ill-fated maiden voyage of the RMS Titanic, which sank in the North Atlantic Ocean after hitting an iceberg in April 1912. It combines historical events with a fictional love story between characters from different social classes.
Director: James Cameron, who also wrote and produced the film. Production: The film was one of the most expensive ever made at the time, with a budget of approximately $200 million. It was praised for its groundbreaking special effects and attention to historical detail. Awards: Titanic won 11 Academy Awards, including Best Picture and Best Director.

Present Day In 1996, treasure hunter Brock Lovett (Bill Paxton) leads an expedition to recover artifacts from the wreck of the Titanic. During their search, they find a drawing of a young woman wearing a necklace known as the Heart of the Ocean. This discovery leads them to centenarian Rose Dawson Calvert (Gloria Stuart), who claims to be the woman in the drawing.
